## Why People Love HelloFresh (The Fair Part)

Before we talk about the problems, let's be honest about why HelloFresh works for so many families.

**The decision is made.** You open the box, you have a recipe, you have ingredients. There's no
trip to the store, no "what are we making tonight?" debate. That friction removal is genuinely
valuable.

**The recipes are reliable.** HelloFresh has a strong recipe development team. Dishes are tested
and calibrated to work for home cooks. When you follow the card, dinner happens.

**Portion control is built in.** Pre-measured ingredients mean less food waste and fewer "I bought
cilantro for one recipe and threw out the rest of the bunch" moments.

**It reduces grocery trips.** Three nights covered by the box means three fewer "what do we need?"
decisions at the store.

These are real advantages. If HelloFresh is working for your family and you can afford it, there's
nothing wrong with staying. The math question is whether you're getting $564 of value per month —
or whether those same outcomes are available for a fraction of the price.

## Why Families Cancel HelloFresh

HelloFresh doesn't broadcast its churn rate, but Reddit threads about canceling it are some of the
most active in meal planning communities. The complaints cluster around four themes:

**The cost creeps up after the intro offer.** Most people start HelloFresh on a heavily discounted
trial (40–60% off the first few boxes). Then full pricing kicks in. The bill that arrives feels
different from the one they signed up for.

**You still need groceries anyway.** The box covers 2–4 dinners. A family of four still needs
breakfast, lunch, snacks, and the other 3–5 dinners per week. So HelloFresh doesn't replace the
grocery trip — it just adds to the grocery bill.

**The portions don't always match a real family.** Recipes are calibrated for 2 or 4 servings.
Families with growing teenage kids, or with one picky eater who won't touch the Thai peanut bowl,
find themselves short — or wasting food.

**Flexibility is limited.** If travel, illness, or a busy week disrupts dinner plans, pausing or
skipping boxes requires advance notice (usually 5 days). Miss the window and a box ships regardless.

**The novelty fades.** HelloFresh rotates recipes, but it's a finite catalog. After 6–12 months,
many families report seeing repeats. The variety that justified the price in month one isn't
there in month twelve.
