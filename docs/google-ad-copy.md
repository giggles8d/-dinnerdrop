## Overview

Three ad groups, three RSA variants each = 9 total responsive search ads.

| Ad Group | Budget Share | Keywords Targeted |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Group 1: Meal Planning Apps | 40% (~$2/day) | meal planning app, meal planner app, grocery list app, weekly meal plan, dinner planner |
| Group 2: AI Meal Planner | 35% (~$1.75/day) | AI meal planner, AI meal planning app, smart meal planner, meal planning AI |
| Group 3: Family Dinner Planning | 25% (~$1.25/day) | meal planning for families, family dinner planner, weeknight dinner ideas, meal planner for busy moms |

**Launch sequence:**
- Week 1: Deploy RSA variants A only (one per group) — 3 ads total
- Week 3: Add RSA variants B (after Google has rotation data)
- Month 2: Add RSA variants C or replace lowest performer

**Google RSA format rules:**
- Headlines: up to 15 per ad, max **30 characters** each (including spaces)
- Descriptions: up to 4 per ad, max **90 characters** each (including spaces)
- Google tests combinations and shows: 3 headlines + 2 descriptions per impression
- **Pinning:** Pin H1 = brand/product name always in position 1. Pin H2 = top value prop always in position 2.
- Let H3–H15 rotate freely — Google optimizes based on CTR

## Pinning Strategy Summary

**Why we pin H1 and H2:**
- H1 always establishes product identity (DinnerDrop) — critical for brand recognition and Quality Score
- H2 always delivers the single strongest value proposition for that ad group
- H3–H15 rotate freely — Google's ML finds the best combination over 2–3 weeks
- Never pin H3 — kills Google's ability to optimize

## A/B Test Framework — Week 1

**Primary test:** Headline framing — does "speed" or "beta/free" perform better?
- 1-A vs 2-A: "5 Dinners in 30 Seconds" (speed) vs "5 Dinners Planned by AI" (AI authority)
- Decision point: Day 7 — keep headline style with higher CTR across both groups

**Secondary test:** CTA framing in descriptions
- "Try free" vs "Free 7-day trial" — monitor across all 3 active RSAs
- Decision point: Day 14 — swap out lower-performer in all 3 groups

**Watch for:** Ad Strength score in Google Ads interface
- "Poor" → add more unique headlines (avoid repetition across H slots)
- "Good" → maintain, add negative keywords if irrelevant searches triggering
- "Excellent" → scale budget +20%
