## 2. Mealime — Best Free Option for Couples and Small Families

**Price:** Free (Pro: $5.99/month)
**Platforms:** iOS, Android
**Best for:** Couples and families of 2-3 who want guided recipe selection

Mealime has been around since 2016 and has built a loyal following for good reason. The
interface is clean, the recipe library is solid (500+ tested recipes), and it produces a
shopping list organized by grocery store section.

The planning flow: each week, you pick from a curated selection of recipes based on your
dietary preferences (set during onboarding). Mealime adjusts serving sizes automatically and
builds the consolidated shopping list.

**What works:**
- Beautifully photographed recipes with clear step-by-step instructions
- Dietary filters: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free, paleo, keto, and more
- Shopping list organized by store section (no zigzagging the aisles)
- Free tier is genuinely useful — not crippled

**What doesn't:**
- No grocery cart handoff — the shopping list stays in the app; you shop manually
- Recipe selection still requires you to browse and choose each week
- Less useful for families of 4+ (larger serving-size adjustments can get off)
- Pro version needed for full recipe library and meal history

**For families:** Mealime works best when adults in the household like most foods and dietary
complexity is low. If you have picky kids or strict dietary needs, filtering gets complicated.

**Price verdict:** The free tier is genuinely useful and worth trying. Pro at $5.99/month is
fair if you use it consistently.

## 3. Plan to Eat — Best for Recipe Collectors Who Want to Stay Organized

**Price:** $8/month or $60/year
**Platforms:** Web, iOS, Android
**Best for:** Home cooks with a large personal recipe collection

Plan to Eat is less of a meal planner and more of a recipe management system with meal
planning on top. The core feature: you import recipes from any website (browser clipper),
they live in your personal library, and you drag them onto a calendar to plan your week.

If you have 200 saved recipes scattered across browser tabs, Pinterest, and three cooking
apps — Plan to Eat is genuinely useful for consolidating them. The calendar drag-and-drop is
satisfying. Shopping list generation from your planned meals is accurate.

**What works:**
- Excellent recipe import (browser extension works on nearly any recipe site)
- Full recipe editing — adjust ingredients, notes, servings
- Shopping list generated from your meal calendar
- Family sharing (multi-user access to the same recipe library and plan)

**What doesn't:**
- You must bring your own recipes — no discovery feature or built-in recipe database
- No AI — completely manual planning every week
- No grocery cart integration — shopping list lives in the app only
- Requires ongoing curation to stay useful as the library grows

**For families:** If one parent is a dedicated cook who loves recipe collections, Plan to Eat
is excellent. If neither parent wants to spend time curating recipes, it will feel like work.

**Price verdict:** $8/month ($60/year) is reasonable for what it does, but the full benefit
requires consistent recipe import over months before it pays off.

## 4. Paprika — Best One-Time Purchase for Serious Home Cooks

**Price:** $4.99 one-time (per platform)
**Platforms:** iOS, Android, Mac, Windows
**Best for:** Dedicated home cooks who want a permanent recipe library

Paprika is the closest thing meal planning apps have to a "classic." It's been around since
2010, charges a one-time fee instead of a subscription, and has a fiercely loyal user base.

Core experience: import recipes from any URL, organize them into categories, scale serving
sizes, and build weekly menus from your library. The grocery list pulls from planned meals
and lets you check off items as you shop.

**What works:**
- One-time purchase (no ongoing subscription fees ever)
- Excellent cross-platform sync (iOS + Mac for the same cook)
- Recipe scaling is accurate and easy
- Large, active community with shared recipe libraries
- Grocery list with aisle organization and check-off feature

**What doesn't:**
- No AI — fully manual planning
- No grocery cart handoff to any stores
- Requires building your recipe library before it becomes truly useful
- Less focused on family dinner planning (more individual power user)
- $4.99 per platform adds up if you want iOS + Mac + Windows

**For families:** Paprika is excellent if one household member is a committed home cook who
wants a premium recipe management experience. It's not designed for quick weekly planning
by two tired parents.

**Price verdict:** $4.99 one-time is exceptional value if you'll actually use it consistently.
Many Paprika users have had it for 5+ years and consider it one of the best value apps
they've ever bought.

## 5. Whisk — Best Free Option for Casual Planners

**Price:** Free
**Platforms:** Web, iOS, Android
**Best for:** Families who want basic planning without committing to a paid app

Whisk (owned by Samsung since 2019) is a free recipe-saving and meal planning app with
reasonable grocery integration. You save recipes from the web, schedule them on a weekly
planner, and generate a shopping list. Whisk has some Instacart integration in select markets.

**What works:**
- Completely free
- Good recipe import from any URL
- Basic meal calendar with drag-and-drop
- Instacart integration available in some regions

**What doesn't:**
- No AI — fully manual recipe selection
- Grocery integration is spotty depending on region
- Recipe database is more limited than Mealime
- Samsung ownership creates some uncertainty about long-term app direction

**For families:** Whisk is a solid free starting point if you want to try meal planning before
committing to a paid app. If you use it consistently, you'll likely outgrow it within a few
months and want more features.

**Price verdict:** Free. Worth trying as a no-commitment starting point.

## 6. Prepear — Best for Visual Planners

**Price:** Free (Pro: $7.99/month or $59.99/year)
**Platforms:** iOS, Android
**Best for:** Families who plan visually and share meal prep between partners

Prepear is a recipe-focused meal planner with a strong visual design. The weekly view is
calendar-style with recipe photos filling each slot — genuinely satisfying to look at and
share. The shopping list pulls from your planned meals. Pro adds pantry tracking to reduce
food waste.

**What works:**
- Beautiful visual meal calendar with recipe photos
- Good for coordinating meal prep between two partners
- Pantry tracking in Pro reduces food waste
- Clean, intuitive interface

**What doesn't:**
- No AI planning
- No grocery cart handoff to stores
- Free tier is limited (fewer recipe saves, no pantry tracking)
- More focused on the visual experience than planning speed

**For families:** Prepear works well when both parents are involved in cooking decisions and
want to align on the week's plan visually. Less suited for solo planners who want speed.

**Price verdict:** Free tier for testing; $7.99/month for Pro is on the higher end compared
to alternatives with similar feature sets.

## 7. Cozi — Best if You Already Use It for Family Scheduling

**Price:** Free (Gold: $29.99/year)
**Platforms:** Web, iOS, Android
**Best for:** Families who want one app for calendar, lists, and meal planning together

Cozi isn't a dedicated meal planning app — it's a family organizer with meal planning as
one of its features. The meal planning component lets you schedule dinners on a shared family
calendar and build a shopping list from planned meals.

If you already use Cozi for family scheduling (school events, activities, appointments), the
meal planning integration is convenient. As a standalone meal planner, it's limited.

**What works:**
- Shared family calendar with meal planning integrated in one place
- Shopping lists shared in real time across all family members
- Family Journal for capturing recipes and memories
- Free tier is quite capable for basic use

**What doesn't:**
- Meal planning is secondary to calendar — limited depth
- No AI, no recipe database, no grocery cart handoff
- Adding recipes requires manual entry or web clipping
- Not useful if you don't already use Cozi for scheduling

**For families:** Cozi is best for families already invested in its ecosystem. As a meal
planning tool on its own, it's outclassed by every other app on this list.

**Price verdict:** Free tier is worth it if you use Cozi already. Not worth starting with
Cozi solely for meal planning.
